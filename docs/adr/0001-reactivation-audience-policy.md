# Reactivation audience policy: cohort-split with active-payer carve-out

The two reactivation campaigns target old free members (Free silent, ~15k —
churned, $0 LTV) and old paid members (paid-churned, ~1.8k — churned,
LTV>0); both push to Build Room. Currently-paying recurring members (~250)
are deliberately **not** emailed, on the theory that they're inertial
non-cancellers and any contact risks reminding them to cancel — revisit
only after the new product is established and revenue is stable. Lifetime
AutomationFlow Pro buyers (~90) get a one-shot transition campaign
(3 months free Build Room) instead of reactivation copy, because they paid
for a now-archived product and have a moral claim to a transition path
distinct from "come back" messaging.

The recurring educational digest (planned, not yet built) follows the same
carve-out — engaged users only, active payers excluded — even though a
value-add educational send is *defensible* to send to active payers. The
consistency of the "don't touch active payers" rule across all email
threads is intentional and load-bearing for the reasoning above.
